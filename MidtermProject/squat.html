The Back Squat

Intermediate

Description

Back squats are a common strength training exercise that primarily targets the muscles of the lower body, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. To perform a back squat, you start by standing with a barbell resting on your upper back, then lower your body by bending your hips and knees until your thighs are parallel to the ground, before driving through your heels to return to the starting position. Back squats can be performed with varying levels of weight and are often used by athletes and fitness enthusiasts to improve overall lower body strength and power.
